Our History

From informal gathering to incorporated association

For years, Lake Julia property owners gathered informally each summer to discuss shared concerns and enjoy the lake together. In 2004, that tradition was formalized when the group incorporated as the Lake Julia Association, Inc. (LJA), a Wisconsin Non-Stock Corporation, organized under articles and bylaws drafted by attorney Thomas J. Drought (#5, #6, #10).

The Corporation is comprised of Members, loosely defined as persons owning property or residing in homes located on the shoreline of Lake Julia and other persons supporting the general purposes of the Corporation, as well as the LJA Board, made up of elected directors and officers (President, Treasurer, Secretary, Technology Officer, and Advisors), who together govern the Association on behalf of its members.

Governance

How we're organized

Members

All shoreline property owners and regular lake users. Members elect the Board of Directors and vote on bylaw amendments (requiring a 2/3 majority).
