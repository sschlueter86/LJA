Overview

A Lake Worth Protecting

Lake Julia is something rare — a deep, clear Northwoods lake that has remained in exceptional ecological health, thanks in large part to the land surrounding it and the people who care for it. A comprehensive management study conducted by Onterra LLC between 2010 and 2013 confirmed what residents have long known: when compared to similar lakes across Wisconsin and the broader Northern Lakes and Forests ecoregion, Lake Julia ranks as having excellent water quality. The reason, in large part, is the watershed itself. Nearly 90% of the 5,363 acres draining toward Lake Julia is wetlands and forest — land that filters and absorbs surface runoff rather than channeling nutrients and sediment into the water. The study found the lake's aquatic plant community to be among the richest documented anywhere in the state, with 58 native species and a Floristic Quality Index nearly double the regional median — a level that scientists associate with pristine, largely undisturbed conditions.

Lake Julia is also, by its nature, a quiet lake. It is not a thoroughfare. There is no chain connection that draws casual, motorized-boat traffic through, and while a public boat ramp exists, it is neither easy nor convenient — the kind of access that tends to attract serious anglers and lake lovers rather than day-trippers. That natural insularity is part of what keeps it special. The 49 property owners who share its shoreline have understood this for generations — half the properties have been in the same families for decades, and that continuity of care shows. The Lake Julia Association exists because good stewardship doesn't happen by accident. It takes investment, vigilance, and neighbors who show up.
