Overview

A Lake Worth Protecting

Lake Julia is something rare: a deep, clear Northwoods lake in exceptional ecological health. A comprehensive management study conducted by Onterra LLC between 2010 and 2013 established it among Wisconsin's top-tier lakes, with excellent water quality, 58 native aquatic plant species, and a Floristic Quality Index nearly double the regional median.

A follow-up study of the lake between 2025 and 2026 confirmed the lake is holding steady or improving: water clarity has increased from 5.9 to 7.2 feet, nutrient and algae levels remain excellent, and no aquatic invasive plants were found. The primary reason for such resilience is the watershed - nearly 90% of the 5,363 acres draining into the lake is wetlands and forest, land that filters runoff rather than channeling nutrients and sediment into the water.

Lake Julia is also, by nature, a quiet lake. Its connection to the Three Lakes chain is shallow and narrow, drawing little to no motorized traffic. The public boat ramp exists but is neither easy nor convenient to use, the kind of access that tends to attract serious anglers rather than day-trippers. That natural insularity is part of what keeps it special. The 49 property owners who share its shoreline have understood this for generations. Half the properties have stayed in the same families for decades, and that continuity of care shows. The Lake Julia Association exists because good stewardship doesn't happen by accident. It takes investment, vigilance, and neighbors who show up to participate.
